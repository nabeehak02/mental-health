Depression

What is Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. It daily affects the way you feel, your ability to do normal activities, and it can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems. Common symptoms of depression include feelings of sadness, tearfulness, or hopelessness, angry outbursts, sleep disturbances, lack of energy, reduced appetite, anxiety, agitation, and frequent or recurrent thoughts of death and suicidal thoughts. About 80% of people experiencing depression are able to return to their regular activities by getting help from professionals or the people around them.

How it Affects Youth

According to the Canadian Mental Health Association, 5% of male youth and 12% of female youth, from age 12 to 19, have experienced a major depressive episode. There are 3.2 million 12-19-year-olds in Canada that are at risk of developing depression. Symptoms of depression can be different in adults than in children and teens. For younger children, symptoms can be clinginess, sadness, irritability, and worry. In teenagers, symptoms of depression include feeling misunderstood, self-harm, loss of interest in normal activities, avoidance of social interactions, poor performance and attendance at school, excessive use of recreational drugs or alcohol, and feelings of negativity.
